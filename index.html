Summary

I've worked for the past decade at the burgeoning intersection of behavioural sciences and artificial intelligence/machine learning, building on my background in computational modelling and mathematical theory building from previous training in the physics of complex systems (for computational neurosience) and in statistical biophysics (for immunology).

Previously within the field of neuroAI, and presently more towards applied psychology and sociology, I seek quantiative answers to scientific questions about

  • inference and decision-making in the behaviour of human individuals and groups
  • complex system dynamics of interacting learning agents.
My current research aims to understand how belief formation impacts social dilemmas and alignment problems in service of improving commmunication and pubic discourse, in particular with regards to effective sustainability transition policy addressing the climate emergency (see climate actionable). Towards this end, I coordinate interdisciplinary collaborations with domain experts in which I contribute my expertise in mathematical theory for decision-making agent learning, the machine learning of their model parameters, and a whole lot of data wrangling.
